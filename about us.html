About Jhapa District

Welcome to Jhapa – Nepal's fertile eastern gateway, where lush green plains meet rich cultural heritage and warm Terai hospitality.

Our Identity

Jhapa District (झापा जिल्ला) is the easternmost district of Nepal, situated in Koshi Province. It covers 1,606 km² of fertile alluvial plains in the Outer Terai region.

With a population of approximately 998,000 (2021 census), Jhapa ranks among Nepal's most populous districts. It borders India to the south and east, serving as an important trade and cultural bridge.

Geography & Natural Beauty

Known as Nepal's "granary," Jhapa is blessed with rich soil irrigated by rivers like the Mechi, Kankai, and Ratuwa. It leads the nation in tea production (over 10,000 hectares under cultivation) and excels in rice, jute, and vegetables.

The landscape features vast paddy fields, wetlands, and the scenic Chure foothills, offering peaceful rural beauty and biodiversity.
